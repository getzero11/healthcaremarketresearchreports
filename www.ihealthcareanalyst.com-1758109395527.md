## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Smart healthcare systems are able to sense and diagnose complex situations, as they are “predictive”, and have the capability to decide and help to decide as well as to interact with the environment. With adoption of smart healthcare systems, healthcare organizations can provide cost-effective quality healthcare services with less IT set-up costs and reduced risk. Smart healthcare systems are critical in driving innovations in the field of medical technology, as they provide the basis for information-based care and cure. The integration of microsensors and micro-actuators in products provide better treatment for patients in the hospital and at home. The seamless linking of microsystems to a telemetric and telediagnostic infrastructure significantly reduces response time, and simultaneously contribute to containing public healthcare costs.
