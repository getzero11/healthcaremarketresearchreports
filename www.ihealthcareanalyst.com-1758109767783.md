## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Vaccination of susceptible animals against foot and mouth disease (FMD) is a proven strategy for helping to combat the disease since the virus is highly infectious and can be spread by infected animals. FMD is caused by seven different types of serotypes: O, A, C, Asia 1 and Southern African Territories (SAT) 1, 2 and 3. The major symptoms of FMD are high fever, sudden weight loss, increased death rate in newborn animals, low conception rate, painful lesions in and around mouth, lips and feet, and excessive salivation. Cloven-hoofed animals especially cattle, pigs, sheep and goats are susceptible to FMD. The FMD vaccines traditionally represents the largest share of the veterinary vaccine market worldwide in terms of sales of the entire livestock biological business.
