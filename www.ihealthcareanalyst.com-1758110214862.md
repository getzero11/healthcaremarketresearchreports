## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Clostridium difficile infection (CDI) is typically treated through a combination of antimicrobial therapy and supportive care. The first-line treatment often involves oral antibiotics, such as vancomycin or fidaxomicin, which are specifically effective against C. difficile while sparing healthy gut bacteria. In cases of mild infections, metronidazole may be considered as an alternative. Severe or recurrent infections may necessitate advanced approaches, such as fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT) to restore a healthy intestinal microbiome.
