## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Acute ischemic stroke is characterized by the sudden loss of blood circulation to an area of the brain, resulting in a corresponding loss of neurologic function. Acute ischemic stroke is caused by thrombotic or embolic occlusion of a cerebral artery and is more common than hemorrhagic stroke. Different imaging studies, including computed tomography, magnetic resonance imaging, computed tomographic angiography, magnetic resonance angiography, and perfusion studies, are useful for evaluation of acute stroke. Ischemic stroke is one of the leading causes of long-term disability and a third leading cause of death worldwide.
