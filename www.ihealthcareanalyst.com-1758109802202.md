## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Home healthcare is a wide range of health care services provided at home for an illness or injury. Home health care is usually less expensive, more convenient, and as effective as a hospital or skilled nursing facility. Professional home health care services include a broad range of care and support services (infusion therapy, rehabilitation, respiratory, telehealth and telemedicine services) for disabled, chronically- or terminally-ill patients recovering from a hospital stay and need medical, nursing, social, or therapeutic treatment and/or assistance with the essential daily activities. The major medical devices include diagnostics and monitoring devices (blood pressure, blood glucose, pulse rate, heart rate, temperature and sleep apnea monitors), therapeutic devices (dialysis, insulin delivery. IV equipment, nebulizers and ventilator devices), and mobility assisted devices (cranes, walkers, scooters, etc.) along with medical supplies. Home healthcare offers lucrative business opportunities due to its increased acceptance and adoption, technological advancements and cost-effective alternatives compared to other modalities such as hospital visits.
