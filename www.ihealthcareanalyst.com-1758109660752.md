## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Telemedicine uses telecommunication and information technologies to provide clinical health care at a distance, thus helping eliminate distance barriers and can improve access to medical services in distant communities. Critical care and emergency situations are the primary areas for which telemedicine evolved in practice.

Telemedicine can be broken into three main categories: store-and-forward, remote patient monitoring and (real-time) interactive services involving emergency telemedicine, telenursing, telepharmacy, telerehabilitation, teletrauma care), and specialist care delivery (telecardiology, telepsychiatry, teleradiology, telepathology, teledermatology, teledentistry, teleaudiology, teleophthalmology).

It is also used to save lives in critical care and emergency situations. The telemedicine technologies and services market in developing countries is likely to grow at a faster pace during the forecast period due to advanced technologies, rapid adoption of mobile services and time and cost savings.
