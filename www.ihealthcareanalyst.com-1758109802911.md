## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Video teleconferencing is an effective tool in remote telemedicine management. It can be used for initial consultation with patients from remote locations, and monitoring and following up with discharged patients to track their recovery. Video telemedicine can also reduce cost and beneficial to patients with mobility issues and allows hospitals to network with each other to provide support and consultations. The video telemedicine solutions extend well beyond delivery of care to rural patients by driving clinical efficiency and continuity of care across care settings for better patient outcomes at a faster speed. Besides doctor-to-patient communication, video conferencing is also allowing hospitals to create networks to provide each other with support and consults. Video devices can include videoconferencing units, peripheral cameras, videoscopes, or web cameras. Display devices include computer monitors, plasma or LED TV, LCD projectors, and even tablet computers. Telemedicine technologies help to provide communication with expert physicians over any distance and transmit practically any medical information (abstracts from case history, X-ray pictures, computerized tomography images, ultrasound results, etc.) necessary for making a qualified conclusion.
