## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Albumin is a concentrate of plasma proteins from blood, and it works by increasing plasma volume or serum albumin levels. Albumin is used in treating a variety of conditions, including shock due to blood loss in the body, burns, low protein levels due to surgery or liver failure, and as an additional medicine in bypass surgery.

Albumin as an excipient has wide applications in drug formulation, drug delivery, vaccine manufacturing and as a component of media for the production of vaccines. It is widely used in pharmaceutical and biotechnology products. Most of the albumin used as an excipient is either derived from plasma or produced from recombinant technology.
