## Report Specifications: Market Size, Market Share, Growth Trends, Forecast, Geography

Regulatory affairs in healthcare industry, government and academia are involved with a wide range of products, such as: pharmaceuticals, medical devices, in vitro diagnostics, biologics and biotechnology, nutritional products, cosmetics, veterinary products, etc. Regulation involves extensive evaluation of a particular drug product to ensure protection of public health, promotion of the product, drug registration, marketing authorization, import and distribution, and pharmacovigilance. Regulatory affairs activities often result in considerable increase in the fixed costs for manufacturing as well as R&D companies for maintaining in-house resources.  Life sciences, medical device and pharmaceutical companies are adopting a blended variable cost approach to optimize their outsourcing costs in spite of full-time staffing costs for distinct set of functions, products and programs. Major benefits that pharmaceutical, medical device and life science companies can recognize from regulatory affairs outsourcing include refocusing of staff on core competency and other key strategic activities, and cost effectiveness.
